Euclid HOPE Task Force - Visioning Session, May 17 2025
Group: Youth Safety
Facilitator: Kathleen McDonnel | Note Taker: Sierra Stallworth
Participants: Adelynn, Jason, Damian Calvert, Sierra, Aris, Herman, Parris, Lindsay, Pam, Naraya

The group envisions more activities, sports, mental health services, and support for youth and their grown-ups. Public positive reinforcement, parenting classes, more youth engagement, community sponsors for youth, acceptance and welcoming of diversity, equality and inclusion, help with transportation, city council meetings with youth speakers, healing circles led by youth, youth ambassadors, community business involvement, and a recreation center were all named as priorities.

Kathleen McDonnel is a longtime youth advocate in Euclid with deep relationships across school and rec center networks. She brings facilitation skills, community trust, and connections to city leadership.

Sierra Stallworth is a young resident and emerging community leader who brings lived experience as Euclid youth, note-taking skills, and a network of peers.

Damian Calvert has been organizing youth sports leagues informally in the neighborhood for three years. He knows which kids are falling through the cracks and has relationships with their families.

The Euclid Boys League has been running youth athletics in the city for over 20 years and serves as a weekly gathering point for boys ages 8-16. Elevated Dance Headquarters offers after-school dance programming rooted in hip hop and African dance traditions that have been part of Euclid's cultural fabric for over a decade. The Upstage Players theater group has a 15-year tradition of producing community theater with youth cast members, drawing on Euclid's rich history of neighborhood storytelling.

Parris noted that there is an informal tradition in her neighborhood of elders hosting front porch gatherings every summer where older residents share stories about what Euclid used to be like, passing down neighborhood history to younger generations.
