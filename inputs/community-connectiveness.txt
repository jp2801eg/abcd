Euclid HOPE Task Force - Visioning Session, May 17 2025
Group: Community Connectiveness and Problem Solving
Facilitator: Danielle | Note Taker: Martha Nieset
Participants: See attached. Theme: Euclid of Our Dreams.

Vision: Solve our own problems, collective resources, community feels good and has its stuff together. School staff has resources to do what is needed. Youth panel to identify issues going on. Youth coalitions that solve their own problems and have resources and support of where to go. Welcoming environment in our neighborhood. Lots of activities, diverse participation. Peaceful. "I love this place" is commonly heard. All community members feel safe.

Goals: Activities are accessible to all the youth of Euclid. Swim and chill spots. Churches that could be used to help support this. Community transportation. Sims Park music and food trucks. Citywide communication. Street meetings, localized groups in neighborhoods. City offers $250 to organize in your neighborhood. To commit to systems of connectivity at the neighborhood level. Euclid Collaborative comprising a website and booklet of all resources in Euclid.

Organizational partners: Schools, Euclid Collaborative, Youth Council in Euclid, Resource Clearinghouse.

Many of the Hope Task Force committee organizations are committed. Police force will refer people to resource center.

Julie Ilcin is a resident with deep roots in Euclid's neighborhood association network. She brings organizing experience, relationships with block club leaders across the city, and knowledge of city government processes.

Martha Nieset is a longtime resident and community connector. She has relationships across faith communities, schools, and civic organizations, and brings experience in community documentation and storytelling.

Kay O'Donnell is the Director of the Euclid Hunger Center, bringing institutional resources, a network of food access partners, and knowledge of which families are most in need across the city.

Stephen Langel works in mainstream public media and brings communications skills, media relationships, and the ability to amplify community stories to wider audiences.

Corey Russell is the Principal of Euclid High School, bringing institutional authority, relationships with hundreds of families, access to school facilities, and knowledge of youth needs.

Demetrius Williams is a Youth Ambassador bringing firsthand knowledge of what youth in Euclid need, peer networks, and the energy and credibility to engage young people.

The Euclid Democratic Club has been a civic organizing hub for decades, with strong turnout infrastructure and relationships across the city's neighborhoods. Neighbor Circles through the Mayor's Office create structured spaces for residents to connect with city leadership. Schools in Euclid serve as trusted anchors in every neighborhood and have gymnasiums, meeting rooms, and green spaces available for community use. Sims Park is a beloved gathering space where summer concerts and cultural events have been held for generations, rooted in a tradition of outdoor community celebration that dates back 40 years.

Deb Kroemer is a resident with community history expertise, having documented neighborhood stories and transitions in Euclid over three decades. She is a living archive of the city's cultural memory.
