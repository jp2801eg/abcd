Attendees noted that Rosa Mendez, a retired nurse, has been offering free health screenings out of her garage every Saturday, and that the Hillside Community Center has agreed to host a new youth coding club starting next month. The neighborhood's longtime community garden on Elm Street provides fresh produce to over 40 families weekly and has become a hub for resident organizing. Local business owner Darnell Pope of Pope's Hardware has donated tools and lumber for the upcoming park bench project, and the Parks & Recreation Department confirmed it will provide matching funds and a permit. Residents also agreed to form a new block association to coordinate snow removal and safety walks, building on the informal mutual aid network that already connects neighbors across the ward. Several longtime residents spoke about the annual Día de los Muertos celebration that the Mendoza family has organized in the park for over 20 years, which draws hundreds of neighbors and preserves traditions brought from Oaxaca. A local elder, Miss Charlene Booker, has been documenting oral histories from families who settled the ward during the Great Migration, and her recordings are now archived at the branch library.
