Euclid HOPE Task Force - Visioning Session, May 17 2025
Group: Trauma and the Cycle of Violence
Facilitators: Mary Gay, Barbara Owens | Note Taker: Mary Gay
Participants: Rachel (Euclid resident 4 years), Karla (Euclid PL 29 years), Jaylen (youth, 14, CCLE), Michael (ally resident), Bev (ally resident, CPL), Trisha (ally resident), Chip (ally resident)

Vision: We envision Euclid as a connected community that interrupts violence and fear collectively and acknowledges the effects of trauma on its members. Less people with guns. More safety than violence. Connections lead to more integration. Mentorship. Services readily available. Acknowledgement from the community. Community members able to recognize hidden trauma.

Goals: Build a relationship with city officials planning the Wellness Center to develop trauma-informed program offerings. Develop a Euclid Hope Ambassador Program to act as contacts in the community knowledgeable about Euclid Hope and current trauma-informed offerings.

Michael is committed as a community member advocate. Bev will discuss Euclid Hope Ambassador capacity.

Resources and assets named: beach clubs, Neighbor Night.

Karla has worked at the Euclid Public Library for 29 years and has deep institutional knowledge of community resources and a trusted presence with longtime residents. She brings reference expertise, community relationships, and library programming experience.

Mary Gay is a licensed social worker with expertise in trauma-informed care, community facilitation, and connection to the Euclid development community.

Cleveland Peacemakers Alliance brings conflict resolution training and violence interruption programming with a track record across multiple Northeast Ohio cities. Life Anew Behavioral Health provides trauma-informed counseling and recovery services. The Euclid Hospital Emergency Department serves as a first point of contact for many residents experiencing crisis and has begun piloting community health worker follow-up. Moore Counseling and Mediation offers culturally responsive mental health services. Village of Healing is a community-based organization providing peer support for trauma survivors. BEST Community Resource Center connects residents to wraparound services.

Euclid's annual Healing Walk, a tradition started 8 years ago by community members who lost loved ones to violence, draws hundreds of residents each spring in a march through the city's neighborhoods as an act of communal grief and hope.
