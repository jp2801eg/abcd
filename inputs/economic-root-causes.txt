Euclid HOPE Task Force - Visioning Session, May 17 2025
Group: Social Economic Root Causes
Facilitator: Marva | Note Taker: Makva
Participants: Willis Brown, Emily, Holody, Karen Brown, Jocelyn Boyd, Dawnia Honna, Ken Feelito, George, Novotny, Taneika, Hill, Andre Brice, John, Wojtila

Vision: We want to live in a city where everyone thrives, barriers and silos are dismantled, and a deep sense of community and connectedness unites us all.

Goals for the next year: community block party, daily newspaper blast, social groups.

The Kappas and Kiwanis are on board to support a block party. Folks can begin with a small block party to get to know their immediate community and then expand to larger gatherings.

Willis Brown is a resident and longtime small business owner on Euclid Avenue. He has deep knowledge of the local economy, relationships with the Chamber of Commerce, and has mentored several young entrepreneurs in the city.

Andre Brice runs an informal job readiness program out of his home, connecting unemployed residents with local employers. He has placed over 30 people in jobs in the last two years.

Cheryl Mays is both a resident and business owner. She operates a catering business that sources food from local suppliers and regularly donates meals to community events.

Kappa Alpha Psi fraternity has a chapter in Euclid with active community service programming including scholarship funds and youth mentorship.

Kiwanis Club of Euclid has been organizing community service projects and fundraisers for 30 years, with strong institutional connections to local schools and government.

Pope's Hardware on Euclid Avenue has been family-owned for three generations and donates tools and materials to neighborhood improvement projects. The Euclid Hunger Center provides food assistance and nutrition resources to families across the city. Euclid's annual Juneteenth block party, organized by a coalition of Black-owned businesses and residents, is a 12-year tradition that celebrates African American heritage and spotlights local entrepreneurs.
